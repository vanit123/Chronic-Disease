CHRONIC DISEASE AND THEIR PECULAR NATURE

What is chronic disease ... ?

A chronic disease, also known as a chronic condition or non-communicable disease (NCD), is a long-lasting or recurrent health condition that persists over an extended period, typically for three months or more. Chronic diseases often develop and progress slowly and can affect a person's overall well-being and quality of life. They can result from a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors.

Common examples of chronic diseases include:

  1. Cardiovascular diseases (e.g., heart disease and hypertension)
  2. Diabetes
  3. Chronic respiratory diseases (e.g., chronic obstructive pulmonary disease - COPD)
  4. Chronic kidney disease
  5. Cancer
  6. Neurological conditions (e.g., Alzheimer's disease)
  7. Autoimmune diseases (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis)
